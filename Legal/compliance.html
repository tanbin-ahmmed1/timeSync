Compliance - TimeSync

At TimeSync, we are committed to ensuring our medical appointment management system operates in full compliance with relevant laws, regulations, and standards. This page outlines our key compliance efforts.

1. Data Protection and Privacy

We prioritize the security and privacy of your data. Our system is designed to comply with:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): For users within the European Economic Area (EEA), we adhere to the principles of GDPR, ensuring lawful, fair, and transparent processing of personal data. This includes implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, providing users with rights regarding their data (access, rectification, erasure, etc.), and ensuring data processing activities have a legal basis. For more information, please refer to our Privacy Policy.
  • Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA): For protected health information (PHI) of users in the United States, we strive to comply with the administrative, physical, and technical safeguards required by HIPAA. This includes implementing security measures to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI.
  • Local Data Protection Laws: We are also committed to complying with other applicable data protection laws in the jurisdictions where our system is used.

2. Accessibility Standards

We believe in making our system accessible to everyone, including individuals with disabilities. TimeSync is designed with adherence to:

  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG): We strive to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards to ensure that our web application is perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for users with disabilities. Our commitment includes providing text alternatives for non-text content, ensuring keyboard navigability, making content readable and understandable, and maximizing compatibility with assistive technologies.

3. Security Practices

We implement robust security measures to protect your information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. These practices include:

  • Encryption: Utilizing encryption protocols to protect data in transit and at rest.
  • Access Controls: Implementing strict access controls and authentication mechanisms to limit access to sensitive data.
  • Regular Security Audits: Conducting regular security assessments and vulnerability testing to identify and address potential risks.
  • Data Integrity: Employing measures to ensure the accuracy and integrity of your data.
  • Incident Response Plan: Having a defined process for addressing and mitigating security incidents.

For more details on our security measures, please visit our Security page.

4. Ethical Use

We are committed to the ethical use of our system. This includes:

  • Transparency: Being transparent about our data processing practices and providing clear information to our users.
  • Accountability: Taking responsibility for our compliance obligations and ensuring our team is trained on relevant regulations and best practices.
  • User Empowerment: Providing users with control over their data and respecting their rights.

5. Ongoing Monitoring and Improvement

Compliance is an ongoing process. We continuously monitor changes in regulations and best practices to ensure our system remains compliant and secure. We are committed to making necessary updates and improvements to meet evolving standards.
